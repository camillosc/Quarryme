News & Commentary — Q1 2026

Key developments, acquisitions, management commentary, and industry news.

Earnings

Knife River beats Q1 estimates by 37%, reports record backlog

KNF reported $0.56 EPS vs $0.41 consensus, with revenue of $755M beating $727M estimates. Record $1.0B backlog (+38% YoY) driven by Central segment (+83% backlog growth, primarily Texas). Stock gapped down despite beat. — April 16, 2026

Acquisition

Martin Marietta completes largest aggregates acquisition in company history

MLM closed QUIKRETE asset exchange on Feb 23, acquiring ~20M tons/year of aggregates capacity in Virginia, Missouri, Kansas, and BC. Divested Midlothian cement plant and Texas ready-mix. Revenue guidance raised to $7.16B and EBITDA to $2.43B. — February 2026

Acquisition

Arcosa completes $450M barge business sale, acquires Florida aggregates

ACA sold inland barge business to Wynnchurch Capital for $450M (Apr 1), pivoting to pure construction/infrastructure play. Separately acquired central Florida natural aggregates operation for $60M in March. Now reports in two segments. — March-April 2026

Acquisition

CRH deployed $4.1B in 38 acquisitions in 2025, including $2.1B Eco Material Technologies

CRH enters 2026 with $8.4B available for M&A (cash + facilities). Eco Material deal adds 125+ supplementary cementitious materials sites. Expects ~$200M incremental EBITDA from 2026 M&A activity. — Ongoing

Acquisition

Knife River expands into Utah, Montana with three 2026 acquisitions

Morgan Asphalt Inc. (Feb, Salt Lake City market), Sparrow Enterprises, and Donaldson Brothers Ready Mix (western Montana — 3 aggregate sources, ready-mix, precast). Continuing aggressive growth strategy post-MDU spinoff. — Q1 2026

Industry

Diesel fuel surges 37.8% in March — largest monthly increase since 1990 Gulf War

National average hit $5.29/gallon in March 2026 driven by Iran geopolitical tensions. Producer Price Index for construction materials recorded largest one-month increase in 4 years. AGC warns costs are pressuring contractors. — March 2026

Industry

IIJA funding deadline: Sept 30, 2026 creates urgency for infrastructure spending

Only 47% of IIJA funds obligated ($275B of $568B allocated). Discretionary funds expire Sept 30. CRH notes 50% of highway funds still to be deployed — expects 2026 to be "record year for transportation infrastructure investment." $2.3B in rescissions announced. — Ongoing

Industry

Data center construction emerges as top private demand driver for aggregates

CRH active on 100+ data center sites, within 25 miles of 85% of all US data sites. VMC seeing significant DC bookings. MLM cites "accelerating momentum in data centers and energy." Replacing softness in residential and private non-residential. — Q1 2026

Guidance

VMC Investor Day: targets $4.5–5.0B long-term EBITDA, $20/ton cash gross profit

Vulcan Materials outlined ambitious growth targets at 260-270M tons. 2026 guidance of $2.4-2.6B EBITDA, 1-3% shipment growth, 4-6% pricing. Pending California ready-mix acquisition expected Q2. Expanding from 3 to 9 locations in Charleston/Myrtle Beach. — Q1 2026

Guidance

Arcosa CFO warns Q1 will face "tough comps" due to Northeast winter

CFO Gail Peck noted severe cold and snow impacted Stavola (NY-NJ) operations. Q1 expected to be a lower percentage of full-year EBITDA than prior year. Full-year guidance maintained: $590-640M adjusted EBITDA. Wind towers expect ~25% revenue decline in 2026. — February 2026

Analyst

Mixed analyst signals: JP Morgan downgrades VMC while Citi raises target to $365

JP Morgan moved VMC to Neutral from Overweight (Mar 4). Citigroup countered with Buy and $365 target. B. Riley upgraded MLM to Buy with $700 target (Apr 2). Wells Fargo downgraded CRH to Equal Weight. Sector views diverging on valuation vs. fundamentals. — Q1 2026

Industry

Pit & Quarry: Autonomy, M&A, and safety headline 2026 aggregates trends

Six key trends: (1) Autonomy extending beyond haul trucks, (2) Digital connectivity adoption, (3) Continued M&A platform deals, (4) Pricing over volumes driving profitability, (5) Safety focus after 27% increase in fatalities in 2025, (6) Supply chain digitalization. — January 2026
