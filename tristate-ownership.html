Tri-State Quarry Ownership Map

Who actually owns the aggregate base of the New York metro? Every active hard-rock quarry and major sand & gravel pit in NY, NJ, and CT, color-coded by listed-company parent. Built to make the roll-up thesis legible at a glance: a small number of large publics and a long tail of independent acquisition targets.

Data current May 2026 · Sources: MSHA MDRS, NY DEC / NJ DEP / CT DEEP, company location finders, 10-K filings, M&A press releases

Why this map exists

Five listed acquirers — Arcosa, Amrize, Heidelberg, Vulcan, Martin Marietta — and one dominant incumbent, CRH, control essentially every large hard-rock quarry inside the New York MSA. The map below shows just how concentrated the listed-company side is, and how shallow the bench of remaining independent targets has become.

The October 2024 Arcosa / Stavola deal — $1.2B for three NJ trap-rock quarries, twelve asphalt plants and three recycle yards at ~12× EBITDA — is the comp that reset trap-rock valuations region-wide. Red dots on the map are the Stavola assets that now sit inside Arcosa.

Listed-company parents

Each card aggregates every owned aggregate-producing site we could verify in the tri-state. Production volumes are MSHA-reported tons or company-disclosed where available; otherwise estimated from permitted acreage and reserve life. M&A notes are the most recent material transaction relevant to the regional asset.

Independent & family-owned operators

Single-site and multi-site private operators not owned by a listed parent. These are the acquisition targets the listed side has spent the last decade hunting. Many have no obvious succession plan — Stavola itself was a third-generation family business when Arcosa bought it.
