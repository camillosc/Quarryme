Tri-State Quarry Acquisition Targets

Investment Memo: Top 10 Independent Quarry Operations in New York, New Jersey & Connecticut
Prepared for QuarryMe.com May 2026 Roll-Up Strategy Analysis

Executive Summary

This report identifies and profiles the 10 most attractive independent quarry acquisition targets in the tri-state area (New York, New Jersey, Connecticut) for a quarry roll-up strategy. All targets are family-owned or privately held, with strategic locations near major demand centers, proven reserves, and operational characteristics that would benefit from consolidation and professionalization.

The tri-state aggregate market benefits from exceptional demand fundamentals (proximity to 20M+ population), extreme barriers to new entrants (permitting takes 10-20+ years), and a fragmented base of aging family-owned operators. The recent Arcosa/Stavola transaction ($1.2B, ~12x EBITDA) validates premium valuations in this market.

Company Overview

Established in the 1890s, Weldon Materials is a leading NJ producer of crushed aggregates, hot-mix asphalt, and ready-mix concrete. The Weldon family has owned and operated the Fanwood/Watchung trap rock quarry continuously since the company's founding. Vertically integrated with 2 quarries, 9 HMA plants, 3 ready-mix plants, and 2 contractor supply yards.

Products & Materials

  • Trap rock (basalt) — Watchung quarry, Somerset County
  • Granite — Lake Hopatcong quarry, Sussex County
  • NJ & NY DOT-approved aggregates
  • Hot-mix asphalt, ready-mix concrete

Ownership & Succession

Multi-generational family ownership (Weldon family) since the 1890s. Privately held with no outside investors. As one of the oldest continuously family-operated quarries in NJ, succession planning is a key consideration. Revenue estimated at $10-100M per third-party databases, likely in the $75-100M range given the scale of integrated operations.

Strategic Value

  • Watchung quarry sits in the First Watchung Mountain basalt — premium trap rock deposit
  • Prime location serving northern NJ metro and NYC demand
  • Vertically integrated (aggregates + asphalt + concrete)
  • Two quarries provide material diversification (trap rock + granite)
Vertically Integrated Trap Rock Granite Multi-Gen Family NYC Metro Proximity

Acquisition Thesis

Premier NJ target. The Weldon Watchung trap rock quarry is one of the most strategically valuable hard rock deposits in the state — sitting atop the First Watchung basalt formation with direct highway access to the densely populated northern NJ corridor. The 130+ year family ownership creates natural succession risk. The vertically integrated model (quarries + 9 asphalt plants + 3 concrete plants) provides immediate revenue synergies in a roll-up. Comparable to the Stavola operation that Arcosa acquired for $1.2B, though smaller in scale. This is essentially a "mini-Stavola" opportunity.

Market Context & Roll-Up Rationale

Why Tri-State Aggregates?

The NY/NJ/CT metro area is the most supply-constrained aggregate market in the US. Zoning restrictions and environmental permitting effectively prevent new quarry development (10-20+ year approval cycles). Meanwhile, 20M+ people and $100B+ annual construction spend drive insatiable demand. The result: aggregate prices in the Northeast ($20-25/ton at the quarry gate) are among the highest in the nation, and transportation costs (50-70% of delivered price) create powerful local monopolies. Every operating quarry near NYC is an irreplaceable strategic asset.

Roll-Up Economics

Aggregate industry M&A has averaged 9.4x EV/EBITDA (2024-26), with Northeast/metro-proximate assets commanding 10-12x+. Crushed stone operations typically generate 25-35% EBITDA margins. A roll-up strategy can acquire at 7-9x (smaller, family-owned targets) and create value through: (1) operational synergies (shared equipment, centralized management), (2) pricing power from reduced competition, (3) vertical integration (adding asphalt, concrete, paving), and (4) multiple expansion as the combined platform trades at institutional-grade multiples. The Stavola exit at 12x EBITDA demonstrates the endgame valuation for a well-constructed tri-state platform.

Key Risks

Permitting: Environmental compliance and community opposition are ongoing challenges. Any permit issues at acquired quarries could threaten the investment. Cyclicality: Aggregate demand correlates with construction cycles, though infrastructure spending provides a floor. Competition: CRH/Tilcon, Arcosa (post-Stavola), and Heidelberg are well-capitalized competitors. Succession dynamics: Family owners may have inflated value expectations. Environmental liability: Historical operations may carry remediation obligations.

Demand Tailwinds

IIJA Infrastructure: Federal infrastructure spending is driving record state DOT budgets. Data centers: Massive concrete/aggregate demand for Northeast data center construction. Housing: NJ/CT housing starts recovering. MTA capital plan: $68B MTA capital program drives aggregate demand in NY metro. Climate resilience: Coastal protection, stormwater, and flood mitigation projects require significant aggregate volumes. These tailwinds suggest above-trend demand for the next 5-10 years.
