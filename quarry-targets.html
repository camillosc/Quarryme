Executive Summary

This database profiles 13 independent quarry operations across the NY-NJ-CT tri-state area as acquisition targets for a roll-up platform strategy. The targets range from single-quarry family operations with $1-5M revenue to mid-size multi-site producers approaching $30M revenue. The thesis is straightforward: the tri-state aggregate market is supply-constrained (permitting is near-impossible for new greenfield quarries), demand is structurally supported by NYC metro infrastructure spend, and fragmented ownership among aging family operators creates a generational acquisition window. Recent comp transactions — Arcosa's $1.2B acquisition of Stavola (12x EBITDA) and CRH's acquisition of North American Aggregates — validate premium valuations for strategically located Northeast aggregate reserves. Small independents can be acquired at 6-8x EBITDA, with platform synergies (shared equipment, centralized sales, blended insurance) driving post-acquisition margin expansion of 300-500 bps.

Market Pricing

$20-25/ton

Northeast crushed stone pricing, with trap rock commanding $22-28/ton premium. NYC metro pricing 15-20% above national average due to supply constraints and transportation costs.

M&A Multiples

9.4x EV/EBITDA

Industry average 2024-YTD 2026. Small bolt-ons at 6-8x; strategic acquisitions 12-15x. Stavola traded at 12x on $100M EBITDA. Small independents targetable at 6-8x.

Supply Dynamics

Near Zero

New quarry permits in the tri-state area. Zoning restrictions, environmental reviews, and community opposition make greenfield development virtually impossible near population centers.

Consolidation Wave

Active

CRH acquired North American Aggregates (Perth Amboy, NJ) in Dec 2025. Arcosa closed Stavola ($1.2B) in 2024. Knife River deployed $129M across 6 bolt-ons at 6-8x. Window is now.
